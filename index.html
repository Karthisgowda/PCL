AI-Enabled Adaptive Solar Energy Management System

A proposed system architecture for portable charging devices that combines adaptive MPPT, predictive battery management, and smart multi-device power distribution.

Karthik S Gowda · Harsh Lakhotia · Hanoch Jiji · Ibrahim Sarfaraz Mukadam
Published link Online edition
AI

1 · Problem Statement

Portable solar charging still behaves like a fixed circuit.

Portable devices are essential during travel, field work, emergencies, and rural use, but conventional power banks depend on grid charging.

Solar chargers solve part of the problem, yet many still struggle when sunlight, temperature, shading, and load demand change throughout the day.

10-15%Typical basic solar power-bank conversion range cited in the draft
8-12 hrsFavorable sunlight often needed for partial charging
Core gapLimited intelligence, not only limited panel efficiency

2 · Objective and Contribution

A literature-grounded architecture for smarter off-grid charging.

Architecture

Defines a unified system for adaptive solar harvesting, battery supervision, and output power control.

Integration

Combines AI-based MPPT, intelligent BMS, and USB-C power distribution into one portable design framework.

Academic clarity

Positions the work as a conceptual system design, not a claim of new algorithms or prototype results.

3 · Literature Gap

Traditional methods are useful, but brittle under real-world variation.

Conventional MPPT

P&O and Incremental Conductance are simple, but can settle at local maximum power points under partial shading.

AI MPPT

ANN and DRL approaches can map changing irradiance, temperature, voltage, and current conditions to better control decisions.

Battery estimation

PINN-based battery models can keep SOC and SOH predictions physically consistent under difficult operating conditions.

4 · Proposed System Architecture

Energy flows from solar input to intelligent charging decisions.

PV Panel
Sensor Module
AI Controller
MPPT Converter
BMS + Battery
USB-C Outputs

The AI Energy Management Controller receives environmental, electrical, and battery data, then regulates solar optimization and power distribution in a continuous feedback loop.

5 · AI-Based Energy Management

Three intelligence layers coordinate harvesting, storage, and delivery.

MPPT

ANN first, DRL later

ANN is recommended for first implementation due to fast, deterministic inference; DRL is reserved for future online adaptation.

BMS

Predictive health

PINN-based SOC/SOH estimation can support safer charging decisions and early degradation warnings.

Power

Dynamic allocation

USB Power Delivery negotiation allows priority-aware charging across phones, laptops, wearables, and field devices.

6 · Operating Phases

The system runs as a continuous adaptive loop.

  1. Harvest: PV panel converts sunlight into DC power.
  2. Sense: Irradiance, temperature, voltage, and current are measured.
  3. Analyze: AI estimates available energy and optimal charging parameters.
  4. Optimize: Converter duty cycle is adjusted for maximum power extraction.
  5. Charge and distribute: BMS supervises storage while USB-C outputs receive dynamically allocated power.

7 · Expected Impact

Expected gains come from smarter control, not exaggerated claims.

Higher energy useBetter tracking under changing solar conditions
Longer battery lifeTemperature-aware and health-aware charge management
Reliable off-grid powerUseful for travel, emergencies, field research, and rural access

8 · Applications

Designed for situations where wall power cannot be assumed.

Outdoor adventure Disaster relief Rural electrification Field research Environmental monitoring Daily commuting

The same architecture can serve consumer, emergency, and professional contexts because it adapts to both energy supply and connected-device demand.

9 · Scope, Publication, and Next Steps

The paper is published, but the system remains a conceptual design.

Current scope

No physical prototype, no experimental validation, no new ML algorithm claim, and no regulatory certification claim.

Next work

Build a prototype, train and evaluate models, test under partial shading and multi-device loads, then optimize form factor.
